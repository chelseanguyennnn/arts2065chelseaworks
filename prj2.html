Imagining a Universal Library

The Archive of the After

What happens when knowledge no longer belongs to corporations, governments, or academic gatekeepers but to everyone?

This speculative project imagines “The Archive”, a decentralised network where people around the world store, share, and protect their collective knowledge. In this future, information is free, but truth becomes more complex.

Power shifts from algorithms to communities, and with it, new tensions emerge: Who decides what’s real? Who controls memory when everyone has a voice?

This work critically reflects on our present dependence on platforms like Google and paywalled academic systems, inviting viewers to imagine a slower, more communal, and more fragile internet future.
